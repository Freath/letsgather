How location works

Gather's location model is deliberately narrow, and it's worth being specific about it because it's genuinely more limited than most people assume.

There is no continuous location tracking. Gather does not run in the background monitoring where you are. Your location is only captured once: at the moment you accept a meetup invite. That single point is used to calculate a fair midpoint for the group and to estimate everyone's travel time to candidate venues.

Once you've accepted a meetup, your pin is visible to the other people in that meetup, and only to them, for up to 4 hours after you responded. After 4 hours, your pin disappears automatically, even if the meetup is still upcoming.

Separately, you can choose to save a home location and any number of named saved locations (like "home" or "the office"). These are provided by you directly, are used to help suggest starting points and routes, and are not shown to other users unless you use them as your location when accepting a meetup.

Accessibility information

If you turn on the accessibility preference in your settings, Gather uses it to filter and prioritise venues that are more likely to be accessible to you. Because this can reveal information about a health-related need, UK GDPR treats it as "special category data", which requires a higher bar than ordinary personal data.

This preference is entirely optional, off by default, used only for filtering venue results, and is not shown to other users. We rely on your explicit consent as the lawful basis for processing it [PLACEHOLDER: to confirm with legal review], which means you can turn it off at any time in your settings, and we'll stop using it immediately.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectify data that's inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Erase your data (see "Deleting your account" below for the fastest way to do this).
  • Restrict how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Port your data to another service in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where we rely on consent (such as accessibility filtering or push notifications).

To exercise any of these rights, email support@letsgather.social. We'll respond within the timeframe required by law.

Deleting your account

You can delete your account directly in the app, without needing to contact us. It's a deliberate two-step process: you'll be asked to type "DELETE" to confirm, and then your account and associated data are removed. This cascades across your data, removes your authentication record, and anonymises historical records (such as past meetups involving other people) rather than leaving them attached to your identity. This is how Gather fulfils your right to erasure.

Cookies, local storage and analytics

This website (letsgather.social) doesn't use tracking cookies. If you request early access, your email address is sent to Formspree, a third-party form service, purely to collect it; it isn't used to track you across sites.

Within the Gather app, we don't use third-party tracking or advertising tools. We use first-party analytics, recorded directly within Gather, to understand feature usage and fix problems, as described above. If that changes, for example if we add a product analytics tool in future, we'll update this policy before we do.
